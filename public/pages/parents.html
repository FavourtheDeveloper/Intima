The Importance of Sexual Health Education

Understanding the significance of sexual health education for children is fundamental. It empowers them to make informed decisions about their bodies and relationships, promotes healthy attitudes, and reduces the risk of sexual health issues. Puberty Changes

Puberty brings lots of changes for a young person and for you as a parent too. Your son/daughter is transitioning from child to adult, and you may feel uncertain about how best to support them through the physical, psychological and emotional changes this brings. Try to show compassion for the changes they’re experiencing and reassure them the changes are normal, and that many are only temporary. Explore the resource here for more detail.

Safe Practices in sexual health

Empowering your child with knowledge about safe practices in sexual health is vital for their overall well-being. By discussing contraception options, emphasizing consent, and educating them about sexually transmitted infections, you're equipping them with the tools to make informed decisions. Encourage open conversations about emotional well-being and provide access to healthcare resources, ensuring they feel supported in every aspect of their sexual health journey. Together, we can help them navigate this crucial aspect of their development with confidence and responsibility.

  • Educate your teen about sexual health

Developing a Healthy Relationships

Talk with your child or teen about the qualities an intimate friend or partner should have. Help them look for relationships with shared respect, honesty, loyalty, trust and kindness. Teach them the importance of open communication, respecting boundaries, and nurturing trust. Encourage empathy, understanding, and individual growth within relationships.
